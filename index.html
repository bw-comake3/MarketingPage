Introducing:

Co-Make

Ever get frustrated with that problem in your town or neighborhood that goes ages without being resolved?

About our site!

Overgrown plant issues?

While navigating around your area, you might notice some overgrown vegetation. An example would be a jungle that someone is claiming as their front lawn. If you have a tall vehicle, you might be worried about the top of your vehicle to hit branches that are overhanging the street. Let your community know that this is an issue, you might not be the only person that has these concerns.

Does the city need to step in to make fixes?

While you may just be one person, you can be the one person to make a change in your community. It is hard to get your voice heard to the big wigs at the courthouse. Now with Co-Make, if enough people have the same problems or worries in town, your message is sure to get to the people that need to see it and make changes for the better of the community. Not only that, now your community knows how had the great idea too!

Road issues, potholes, lines not visible?

When driving in town, you might have noticed some of the road need a little bit of extra loving and patch work. There is always that possibility that you might hit an unavoidable pot hole that is sure to cause some damage, if not to your car then to someone elses. Maybe you can not tell what lane is which anymore. Whatever it may be, you will now be able to let the people and your city or town area know about the issues.

*While you see a real change in your community, you get rewards for being a positive community member in the form of points, freebies and local recognition.*

How it works

It take just 3 steps

1. Describe the issue. Could be issues with the road, side walk, buildings, overhanging trees, trees in sidewalks, overgrown lawns. You think it needs addressing, post it to Co-Make.

2. Vote on issues with the highest priority from around your area. Others using Co-Make will vote on what their biggest issues are around the area they live.

3. Problems with the most amount of votes gets prioritezed by local government or HOA's to be resolved first and work down the ladder.
